A.P.J ABDUL KALAM

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, India's "Missile Man," was an aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, celebrated for his role in advancing the country's missile and space programs.

  • Dr. Kalam was instrumental in developing India's space and missile technologies. His leadership earned him the title "Missile Man of India," reflecting his significant contributions to national security.
  • He played a vital role in India's nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. His work in this area was crucial for India's strategic defense capabilities.
  • Serving as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, Kalam was known as the "People's President." He focused on empowering youth and promoting education during his tenure.
